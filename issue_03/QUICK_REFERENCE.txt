KEY DOLLAR AMOUNTS (MEMORIZE THESE)
================================================================================

CONSOLIDATION:
  - 1,164 hospital mergers (2000-2020) among ~5,000 hospitals
  - 238 mergers (20%) predictably anticompetitive; only 13 challenged (1.1% enforcement)
  - Price increases: 1.6% average; 5.2% in high-market-power deals; 7-50% in independent mergers
  - Health spending impact: $204M per merger in year after close

CHARGEMASTER:
  - Median markup: 3.5× costs (250% above)
  - 90th percentile: 6.6× costs (560% above)
  - CT scans: 30× markup ($51 cost → $1,565 charge)
  - Markups increased 155% between 1996-2017

RURAL HOSPITAL CRISIS:
  - 219 closures (Jan 2005 – May 2024)
  - 700+ at risk of closure; 360 imminent (2-3 years)
  - $12 billion in impaired municipal bonds
  - 44% of rural hospitals have negative operating margins

FACILITY FEES:
  - +14% outpatient price inflation when hospitals acquire physician practices
  - Nearly 50% of that 14% increase = facility fee billing
  - Colorado case: $50M+ extra charges for just 25 services

PRICE TRANSPARENCY:
  - Only 21% of hospitals fully compliant (Nov 2024)
  - 46% not fully compliant (HHS OIG 2024)
  - Only 1 billion+ records available from hospital machine-readable files

NONPROFIT TAX EXEMPTION:
  - $28-37 billion/year tax exemption value
  - Only ~$16 billion/year in actual charity care
  - Tax break 1.75× exceeds charity care delivered
  - Range: 0-3% of hospital expenses allocated to charity care

INTERNATIONAL PROCEDURE COSTS:
  - Hip replacement: US $40K vs Germany $14K (2.9× more expensive)
  - Bypass surgery: US $89K vs Spain $10.7K (8.3× more expensive)
  - Knee replacement: US $30-50K vs international $4-6K (6-10× more expensive)
  - Coronary angioplasty: US 2.9× average cost of peer nations
  - Appendectomy: US $21K (25th-95th percentile $9-33K) vs Australia $5.5K

OTHER KEY STATS:
  - 340B drug discount rate: 20-40% below retail; ~$3-5B/year program
  - HRSA audits <10% of hospitals annually for 340B compliance
  - Hospital bond defaults (Q1 2023): highest in 15+ years
  - Private insurers pay 254% of Medicare rates (RAND 2022)
  - 18 states restricting facility fees (as of Sept 2024)
  - 35 states + DC have CON laws; 11% higher costs in CON states (KFF)

POLICY OPTIONS (RANKED BY FEASIBILITY + SAVINGS)
================================================================================

HIGH FEASIBILITY / MODERATE SAVINGS:
  A) FTC Consolidation Enforcement - $30-50B/year
     - Block predictably anticompetitive mergers
     - Build on current FTC momentum
     - Evidence: 1,164 mergers; only 13 challenged

  B) Facility Fee Restrictions - $16-30B/year
     - Ban facility fees for preventive/primary care
     - Connecticut model exists; 18 states moving
     - 14% outpatient price inflation evidence

  C) Transparency Enforcement Expansion - $5-10B/year
     - Strengthen CMS price transparency rule
     - Modest impact; mainly regulatory/informational

MODERATE FEASIBILITY / MODERATE SAVINGS:
  D) 340B Program Enforcement - $1-2B/year
     - Tie participation to 30% charity care requirement
     - HRSA enforcement expansion
     - Lower magnitude but strong moral case

  E) Chargemaster Markup Caps - $10-15B/year
     - Cap at 2.5× actual costs
     - Primarily helps uninsured patients
     - Implementation complexity: cost accounting disputes

LOW FEASIBILITY / HIGH SAVINGS:
  F) Nonprofit Tax Exemption Reform - $12-20B/year
     - Increase minimum charity care to 5% revenue
     - High legal risk; hospital litigation likely
     - Redistributive (not pure cost reduction)

  G) International Reference Pricing - $60-90B/year
     - Medicare rates tied to international median
     - Moonshot: would require legislation + years of litigation
     - Highest savings potential; lowest political feasibility
