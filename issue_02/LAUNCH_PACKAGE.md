### ❌ CRITICAL — Must Fix Before Publishing

**1. Ozempic is listed but not in the analysis**

The draft says: *"The nine drugs — Eliquis, **Ozempic**, Jardiance, Xarelto..."*

**Problem:** The data analysis covers **Imbruvica (ibrutinib)**, not Ozempic. Ozempic (semaglutide) was *not* part of the IRA's first 10 negotiated drugs — it was selected in Round 2 (effective 2027). The first-10 list includes Imbruvica.

**Fix:** Replace "Ozempic" with "Imbruvica" everywhere it appears in the drug list.

> *"The nine drugs — Eliquis, Jardiance, Xarelto, Farxiga, Januvia, Entresto, Stelara, **Imbruvica**, and Enbrel..."*

---

**2. NASA budget comparison is incorrect**

The draft says: *"More than NASA's entire budget."*

**Problem:** NASA's FY2023 enacted budget was approximately **$24.5–25.4 billion**. Eliquis at $18.3B is *less than* NASA's total budget. This is a factual error that will be easy for critics to spot and will undermine credibility.

**Fix options:**
- *"More than the combined budgets of the National Science Foundation ($9.9B) and the FDA ($6.6B)"* — $16.5B < $18.3B ✓
- *"More than the National Cancer Institute's entire budget — twice over"* — NCI ~$7.3B × 2 = $14.6B < $18.3B ✓
- *"Roughly equal to the entire Department of the Interior's annual budget"* — DOI ~$17.6B ✓

Recommended replacement line:
> *"That is more than the US budget for the National Cancer Institute. More than the entire National Science Foundation and FDA combined. One drug, one year."*

---

**3. Eliquis NHS ratio: text says 625×, chart shows 581×**

The draft says: *"the NHS reimburses pharmacies £1.09 — about $1.38 — for a month's supply... Medicare pays 625 times more."*

**Problem:** £1.09 is the NHS Drug Tariff price for a **56-tablet pack** (28-day supply at 2 tabs/day). Normalized to a 30-day supply — which is how all Medicare figures are stated — it is **£1.168 ≈ $1.483**. That gives **581×** (matches the chart).

**Fix:** Change the NHS price and ratio to be consistent with the chart:
> *"the NHS reimburses pharmacies roughly £1.17 — about $1.48 — for a 30-day supply of generic apixaban. Medicare pays **581 times** more."*

---

**4. Rivaroxaban (Xarelto): cited price gives the wrong ratio**

The draft says: *"The UK NHS pays £1.23 — $1.56 — for a month's supply... A 566-fold difference."*

**Problem:** Same 28-day vs 30-day issue. £1.23 is the 28-tablet pack price. 30-day supply = **£1.318 ≈ $1.674**. The 566× figure is *correct* (it comes from the 30-day calculation: $948 / $1.674 = 566×), but the stated price of $1.56 gives 608×, not 566×. The numbers are internally inconsistent.

**Fix:** Use the 30-day figure:
> *"The UK NHS pays roughly £1.32 — about $1.67 — for a 30-day supply of generic rivaroxaban. A 566-fold difference."*

---

### ⚠️ MODERATE — Worth Addressing

**5. "100–600× below Medicare gross" overstates the lower bound**

The draft says: *"UK NHS price (for those with generics available): 100–600× below Medicare gross"*

**Problem:** The actual range from our data is **8.6× to 581×**. Even limiting to drugs where generics are well-established in the UK, Jardiance is only **22×** lower (not 100×). The "100×" floor only holds for the blood thinners (Eliquis 581×, Xarelto 566×) and Januvia (322×) and Farxiga (113×).

**Fix:** Be more precise:
> *"UK NHS price (for those with generics available): from 22× lower (empagliflozin / Jardiance) to 581× lower (apixaban / Eliquis)"*

---

**6. "Eliquis dropped from a $521 per-unit list price" — inconsistent with $862 used elsewhere**

The draft says: *"Eliquis dropped from a $521 per-unit list price to $231 negotiated."*

**Problem:** The newsletter's own data shows Medicare's 2023 average gross cost per claim was **$862**. The "$521" figure appears to be the Wholesale Acquisition Cost (WAC) benchmark CMS used as a starting point in IRA negotiations — a different metric than average Part D gross cost. A reader will reasonably ask "wait, is the price $521 or $862?"

**Fix:** Add brief context:
> *"Eliquis's IRA ceiling price dropped from the $521 negotiation benchmark to $231. The Part D average gross cost per claim — the figure that drove $18.3B in spending — was $862, reflecting dispensing markups and plan variability. The negotiated $231 applies to the CMS benchmark unit."*

Or simplify by just removing the $521 and saying: *"Eliquis's negotiated price is $231 — down substantially from prior levels, but still roughly four times the $59 international average."*

---

**7. "$25B conservative" vs "$48.9B gross" — methodology note doesn't add up**

The draft says gross savings = ~$48.9B and "conservative" = "$25B" after accounting for rebates, while separately saying rebates average "25–30%."

**Problem:** At 30% rebates, $48.9B × 0.70 = **$34.2B** — not $25B. To get $25B requires a ~49% rebate adjustment, which isn't explained.

**Possible explanation:** The $25B figure may apply a rebate reduction to *both* sides — reducing Medicare's actual net cost AND noting that manufacturers would not simply accept the international price without some pushback. If so, this is defensible but needs to be stated explicitly in the methodology note.

**Fix option:** Either change the conservative figure to $34B (30% rebate adjustment, clearly stated), or add a sentence explaining: *"The $25B figure applies a more conservative 45–50% adjustment reflecting the full range of possible rebate rates on high-spend brand drugs, per CMS estimates of 2023 net costs."*
