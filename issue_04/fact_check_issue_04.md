## Flags

### CRITICAL (must fix before publication)

**1. Humalog 2022 list price claim is imprecise**

- **Draft claims:** "By 2022, Humalog's list price was $316 per vial"
- **Actual 2022 price:** ~$275–$300 per vial (sources show 2017 price was $275; sources do not show 2022 price exceeding $300)
- **Source:** Web search results show Humalog at $275 in 2017 and that price remained largely unchanged through 2022. In 2023, Eli Lilly cut the list price by 70% to $66–$86.
- **Assessment:** The "$316" figure is either data error or uses a source not verified. The well-documented 2022 price is closer to $275–$300.
- **Recommendation:** Revise to "By 2022, Humalog's list price was approximately $275–$300 per vial" or find the specific source that documents $316.

### MODERATE (should fix)

**2. Filgrastim 76% claims market share in 2023 not verified**

- **Draft claims:** "biosimilars captured **76% of Medicare Part D claims** in 2023"
- **Available data:** Studies show 56% of *spending* (not claims) captured by biosimilars in 2019. Newer studies from 2023–2025 do not cite a 76% claims figure.
- **Assessment:** This is original author analysis from CMS Medicare Part D data (DY2023), which cannot be independently verified through web search. The filgrastim biosimilar market (Zarxio, Granix, etc.) has been strong, but 76% claims penetration in 2023 exceeds what published literature documents.
- **Recommendation:** (a) Cite the specific CMS data file used and the source worksheet reference, OR (b) add a footnote stating "Author analysis of CMS Medicare Part D Spending by Drug, DY2023" to make clear this is original computation, OR (c) if this is an estimate, reframe as "approximately 70–80% of claims" to signal uncertainty.

**3. PBM industry lobbying claim needs specificity**

- **Draft claims:** "PCMA (the PBM trade association) and its member companies collectively spent well over $100 million lobbying Congress between 2020 and 2024"
- **Verified data:** PCMA alone spent $9M (2022) + $15M (2023) + $18M (2024) ≈ $42M for 2022–2024. Full 2020–2024 with 2020–2021 figures would be higher but specific company-by-company breakdowns not found in web search.
- **Assessment:** The claim is plausible (major health companies like UnitedHealth, CVS, Cigna each spend $40M+ in total lobbying across all issues). However, the specific "well over $100 million" figure for "PBMs and member companies" on healthcare/drug pricing issues only cannot be verified via the sources cited (OpenSecrets, lobbying disclosure).
- **Recommendation:** Either (a) cite specific OpenSecrets data showing the $100M+ figure, (b) reframe as "PCMA and its member companies are estimated to have spent over $100 million lobbying on healthcare and drug pricing issues between 2020 and 2024," OR (c) provide a breakdown of PCMA + individual company spending to show how the total is calculated.

**4. Cigna operating margin rounding — minor precision note**

- **Draft claims:** Operating Margin: 3.8%
- **Actual:** 9417 / 247121 = 3.809% → rounds to 3.8%. This is correct, but just barely. Worth noting the source SEC 10-K filing shows 3.81% when calculated precisely.
- **Assessment:** Not an error, but precision is tight. The figure is correct.
- **Recommendation:** No change required; 3.8% is accurate.
