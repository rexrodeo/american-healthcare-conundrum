## Flagged Issues

### FLAG 1: Arkansas PBM ownership ban — BLOCKED BY FEDERAL COURT
- **Location**: Line 221 (2026 legislation section)
- **Claim in draft**: "Arkansas passed the first state ban on PBM ownership of pharmacies"
- **Issue**: Technically correct that the law passed (Act 624, signed April 16, 2025). HOWEVER, a federal judge (U.S. District Judge Brian Miller) issued a preliminary injunction on July 28, 2025, blocking enforcement. The court found the law likely violates the Commerce Clause and is likely preempted by TRICARE. Arkansas appealed July 31, 2025. The law is NOT currently in effect.
- **Recommended fix**: Change to "Arkansas enacted the first state ban on PBM ownership of pharmacies (Act 624, April 2025), though a federal court issued a preliminary injunction blocking enforcement in July 2025; the state has appealed"
- **Source**: Mintz law firm analysis (Aug 2025), ACHI analysis, NCPA
- **Severity**: WARNING — the claim as written implies the ban is active, which misleads the reader

### FLAG 2: Washington hospital reference pricing — OVERSTATEMENT
- **Location**: Line 275 (What's Next, Issue #3 progress note)
- **Claim in draft**: "three states (Indiana, Washington, and Vermont) enacted hospital reference pricing legislation in 2025, with Washington capping state employee plan reimbursement at 200% of Medicare"
- **Issue**: Indiana and Vermont are confirmed enacted. Washington's situation is less clear-cut: the search results describe Washington as having "proposed" caps ranging from 200% to 350% of Medicare for public employee plans, following Oregon's lead. The HFMA source says states are "advancing" price caps. Need to verify whether Washington's bill was signed into law or is still pending.
- **Recommended fix**: Verify Washington's bill status. If not enacted, change to "two states (Indiana and Vermont) enacted hospital reference pricing legislation in 2025, with at least thirteen additional states (including Washington) considering similar bills"
- **Source**: Milbank, Georgetown CHIR, HFMA
- **Severity**: WARNING — the distinction between enacted and proposed matters for credibility

### FLAG 3: "Thirteen additional states" considering reference pricing

- **Issue**: The Trilliant Health analysis discusses growing state legislative activity but the specific count of "thirteen" is not sourced. The Georgetown CHIR and HFMA articles reference multiple states but don't give an exact count.
